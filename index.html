PAN

Personal Area Network

Coverage Range

1-10 meters - Typically within a person's immediate workspace or personal space.

Designed for communication between personal devices in close proximity.

Speed & Performance

Up to 2 Mbps (Bluetooth Classic)

Up to 50 Mbps (Bluetooth 5.0 with enhanced data rate)

Ultra-wideband (UWB) can reach up to 675 Mbps for high-speed transfers

Technologies
  • Bluetooth - Most common wireless PAN technology
  • Infrared (IrDA) - Line-of-sight communication
  • USB - Wired personal device connections
  • Zigbee - Low-power, mesh networking for IoT
  • Z-Wave - Home automation focused
Common Applications
  • Wireless headphones and earbuds
  • Smart watches and fitness trackers
  • File transfer between mobile devices
  • Wireless keyboards and mice
  • Personal healthcare devices
  • Wireless presenters and controllers

PANs are characterized by low power consumption, simple setup, and device-to-device communication

LAN

Local Area Network

Coverage Range

Up to 1-2 kilometers - Typically confined to a single building or campus.

Can be extended with repeaters and switches, but remains within a limited geographical area.

Common examples: office buildings, schools, hospitals, residential homes.

Speed & Performance

10 Mbps - 100 Gbps depending on technology

Standard Ethernet: 10/100/1000 Mbps (Gigabit)

10 Gigabit Ethernet: 10 Gbps

40/100 Gigabit Ethernet for data centers

Wi-Fi 6/6E: Up to 9.6 Gbps

Technologies & Topologies
  • Ethernet (IEEE 802.3) - Most common wired LAN
  • Wi-Fi (IEEE 802.11) - Wireless LAN standard
  • Token Ring - Older LAN technology
  • FDDI - Fiber Distributed Data Interface
  • Topologies: Star, Bus, Ring, Mesh
Applications & Use Cases
  • Office and corporate networks
  • Educational institution networks
  • Home networks and smart homes
  • Hospital and healthcare facilities
  • Small to medium business networks
  • Gaming LAN parties and events

LANs offer high data transfer rates, low latency, and are typically privately owned and managed

MAN

Metropolitan Area Network

Coverage Range

5-50 kilometers - Typically covers a city or large campus.

Span an area larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN.

Often connects multiple LANs across a metropolitan area.

Examples: City-wide networks, university campuses with multiple locations.

Speed & Technology

10 Mbps - 10 Gbps depending on implementation

Common technologies:

  • Fiber optic cables - High-speed backbone
  • Microwave transmission - Wireless MAN
  • DSL (Digital Subscriber Line)
  • ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode)
  • Ethernet MAN - Metro Ethernet
Architecture & Management

Typically managed by:

  • Internet Service Providers (ISPs)
  • Municipal governments
  • Large organizations with multiple locations
  • Telecommunication companies

Often uses distributed architecture with multiple connection points across the city.

Applications & Implementations
  • City-wide public WiFi networks
  • Cable television networks
  • Interconnecting branch offices of a company
  • University networks across multiple campuses
  • Government agency networks within a city
  • ISP infrastructure for local service delivery

MANs bridge the gap between LANs and WANs, offering city-wide connectivity with moderate to high performance

WAN

Wide Area Network

Coverage Range

Global (unlimited geographical scope) - Spans countries, continents, or the entire world.

The largest type of computer network in terms of geographical area.

Consists of multiple interconnected LANs, MANs, and other WANs.

The Internet is the largest WAN in existence.

Technologies & Infrastructure
  • Fiber optic cables (submarine cables for intercontinental)
  • Satellites for remote or mobile connectivity
  • Microwave transmission for point-to-point links
  • Leased lines (T1, T3, E1, E3)
  • MPLS (Multi-Protocol Label Switching)
  • Frame Relay and ATM networks
  • SD-WAN (Software-Defined WAN)
Key Components
  • Routers - Direct traffic between networks
  • Switches - Connect devices within networks
  • Firewalls - Security between networks
  • Modems - Convert signals for transmission
  • Communication servers - Handle user connections
  • WAN optimization appliances - Improve performance
Applications & Examples
  • The Internet - Global public WAN
  • Multinational corporation networks
  • Global banking and financial networks (SWIFT)
  • Cloud service provider infrastructure
  • Government and defense networks
  • Research and education networks (Internet2)
  • Airline reservation systems

WANs have the highest infrastructure cost, involve multiple administrative controls, and form the backbone of global communications

Key Takeaways

  • Network classification is primarily based on geographical coverage
  • Each network type serves specific use cases and has appropriate technologies
  • Larger networks typically build upon smaller ones (WANs connect MANs, which connect LANs)
  • Modern networks often blend characteristics of multiple types (e.g., SD-WAN, 5G networks)
  • The Internet represents the ultimate WAN, connecting all other network types globally

Network types represent a continuum from personal to global connectivity, each with distinct characteristics and applications
